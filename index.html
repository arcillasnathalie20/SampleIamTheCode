Our Journey

Quirino High School’s iamtheCODE journey began with a mission to promote equal access to technology and empower learners through digital literacy.

  • Program Launch: Orientation and introduction to iamtheCODE.
  • Weekly Coding: Students learned HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
  • Mentorship: Collaboration and guidance sessions with mentors.
  • Completion: Final outputs and reflection activities.

Learnings and Achievements

Technical Skills

  • Web design using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
  • Problem-solving and creativity

Soft Skills

  • Leadership and teamwork
  • Communication and collaboration

Student Testimonials

SDG Project: CodeConnect Community Initiative

Aligned SDGs: SDG 4 – Quality Education | SDG 5 – Gender Equality

A campaign promoting digital literacy and equal opportunities for all learners through coding and collaboration.

  • Peer coding tutorials
  • Mini coding challenges
  • Awareness campaigns

FAQs

What is iamtheCODE?
iamtheCODE is a global movement empowering youth to learn coding and achieve the UN SDGs.
How did Quirino High School implement the program?
Through 12 weeks of coding workshops, mentorship, and collaborative projects.
What skills did students gain?
Technical coding skills, problem-solving, leadership, and teamwork.
How does the program promote education and equality?
By ensuring equal access to digital learning for all students.
